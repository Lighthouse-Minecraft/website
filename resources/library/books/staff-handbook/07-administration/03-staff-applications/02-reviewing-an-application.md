## Overview

This page walks through the hands-on process of reviewing a staff application -- from opening the review list to making a final decision.

## Who Can Do This

Only **Admins** and **Command Department Officers** can take review actions. Other Crew Members can view applications but won't see any action buttons.

## Finding Applications

1. Go to [Admin Applications]({{url:/admin/applications}}) -- you can also find this in the navigation under **Admin**
2. You'll see a table of all applications with the applicant's name, position, department, status, and submission date
3. Use the **status filter dropdown** at the top to narrow down the list (e.g., show only "Under Review" applications)
4. Click **Review** next to any application to open the detail page

## The Review Detail Page

When you open an application, you'll see several sections:

### Applicant & Position Info

Two cards at the top show the applicant's name (linked to their profile) and the position they applied for, including department and rank badges.

### Applicant Background

This card shows a snapshot of the applicant's profile **at the time they applied**:

- **Age** -- if provided
- **Member Since** -- when they joined, with duration
- **Membership Level** -- their level at time of application
- **At Current Level Since** -- how long they've been at that level
- **Reports** -- number of discipline reports
- **Commendations** -- number of commendations received

This data is frozen at submission time. Even if the applicant's profile changes later, this snapshot stays the same.

### Application Responses

All of the applicant's answers to the application questions, displayed as cards with the question and their response.

### Staff Notes

Private notes that are **not visible to the applicant**. Any reviewer can add notes here -- use them for internal observations, concerns, or interview impressions. Type your note in the text area and click **Add**.

### Status Change Notes

A running log of notes added during status transitions. These are appended automatically when you enter notes in any of the action modals.

## Taking Actions

The action buttons change depending on the current application status. Here's what's available at each stage:

### When Status is "Under Review"

- **Schedule Interview** -- Opens a modal where you can add optional notes. This creates an interview discussion and notifies the applicant.
- **Deny** -- Opens a modal for entering a reason. The applicant is notified.

### When Status is "Interview"

- **Move to Background Check** -- Opens a modal to add optional notes and advance the application. Coordinate the actual check using the Background Checks card (see below).
- **Approve without Background Check** -- Skips the background check stage entirely. Opens a modal for optional conditions and notes. The applicant is assigned to the position immediately.
- **Interview Discussion** -- Quick link to the interview discussion thread.
- **Deny** -- Same as above.

### When Status is "Background Check"

The **Background Checks** card appears on this page (if you have the Background Checks - View or Manage role). Use it to add check records, update their status, and upload documents -- all without leaving the review page. See [[books/staff-handbook/user-management/background-checks/managing-background-checks|Managing Background Check Records]] for how the card works.

- **Approve Application** -- Only available once the applicant has at least one terminal background check record (Passed, Failed, or Waived). Opens a modal for optional conditions and notes. The applicant is assigned to the position on approval.
- **Deny** -- Same as above.

If the applicant doesn't have a completed check record yet, you'll see a message instead of the Approve button. Add the check record via the Background Checks card first.

## Discussion Threads

Two discussion links appear below the status card when available:

- **Staff Review Discussion** -- Created automatically at submission. Only staff reviewers are participants. Use this for internal deliberation.
- **Interview Discussion** -- Created when you schedule an interview. The applicant is included as a participant.

Both discussions are automatically closed and locked when the application reaches a final decision (Approved or Denied).

## Tips for Effective Reviews

- **Check the applicant's profile** -- Click their name to see their full profile, including activity and linked accounts
- **Use staff notes** -- They're private and help other reviewers understand your thinking
- **Add notes during transitions** -- The modal notes field creates a running log that other reviewers can follow
- **Don't rush background checks** -- Use the "Update Background Check" button to track progress without having to make a final decision yet
