## Overview

The blog is how we share updates, stories, and news with the community. As a **Blog Author**, you have access to the Blog Management page where you can create posts, edit existing ones, and organize content with categories and tags.

## Who Can Do This

You need the **Blog Author** role to access the Blog Management page. Admins also have full access.

## Getting to Blog Management

There are two ways to get there:

1. From the **Staff Ready Room** -- click the **Blog Management** button in the top-right corner
2. From the public **Blog** page -- click the **Manage** button (only visible to Blog Authors)

## Creating a New Post

1. On the Blog Management page, click **New Post** in the top-right corner
2. Fill in the post form:
   - **Title** -- a clear, engaging headline for the post (required, max 255 characters)
   - **Body** -- the post content in Markdown format (required, minimum 10 characters)
   - **Category** -- choose a category to help readers find related posts
   - **Tags** -- select one or more tags for additional organization
   - **Meta Description** -- a short summary for search engines and social media previews (max 160 characters). If you leave this blank, search engines will pick their own excerpt.
   - **Hero Image** -- the main image displayed at the top of the post. Accepted formats: JPG, PNG, GIF, WebP.
   - **OG Image** -- the image shown when the post is shared on social media. If you don't upload one, the hero image won't be used automatically -- social shares will have no image.
3. Click **Save**

Your post starts as a **Draft**. It won't be visible to the community until it goes through the review and publishing process.

## Writing Post Content

The body field supports **Markdown** formatting:
- Use `**bold**` and `*italic*` for emphasis
- Use `## Headings` to organize sections
- Use `- items` for bullet lists and `1. items` for numbered lists
- Use `[link text](URL)` for links
- Use `![alt text](image-url)` for inline images

Click **Preview** in the post form to see how your Markdown will look before saving.

## Embedding Community Stories

If you want to feature community member stories in your post, you can embed them directly:

1. In the post form, scroll to the **Community Stories** section
2. Select a **Community Question** from the dropdown
3. Check the boxes next to the **approved responses** you want to feature
4. In your post body, the selected stories will be rendered as styled blockquote cards when the post is published

The story authors will be notified when the post goes live that their story was featured.

## Editing a Post

1. Find the post in the **Posts** tab on the Blog Management page
2. Click the **Edit** button in the Actions column
3. Make your changes and click **Save**

You can edit posts in any status. If you edit a post that's already **Published**, an "Edited" badge will appear on it so readers know it was updated after publication.

## Searching and Filtering Posts

The Posts tab has tools to find what you're looking for:

- **Search** -- type in the search box to filter posts by title
- **Status filter** -- use the dropdown to show only posts in a specific status (Draft, In Review, Scheduled, Published, Archived)

## Managing Categories

Categories are broad groupings for posts (e.g., "Community News", "Server Updates", "Guides").

1. Click **Categories & Tags** in the top-right, or click the **Categories & Tags** tab
2. To create a category, click **Create Category** and fill in:
   - **Name** -- the display name
   - **Slug** -- auto-generated from the name, but you can customize it
   - **Include in Sitemap** -- toggle on if you want this category page indexed by search engines
3. Click **Save**

You can't delete a category that still has posts assigned to it. Reassign or remove the category from those posts first.

## Managing Tags

Tags are more specific labels that can be applied across categories (e.g., "events", "builds", "community-spotlight").

1. Go to the **Categories & Tags** tab
2. Type a tag name in the input field and click **Create**
3. To delete a tag, click the delete button next to it. Deleting a tag removes it from all posts automatically.

## Deleting a Post

You can delete a post if you're the original author, a Command Officer, or an Admin. Click the **Delete** button in the Actions column on the Posts tab. Deleted posts are soft-deleted -- they show a "This post has been removed" message to anyone who visits the old URL, rather than disappearing completely.
