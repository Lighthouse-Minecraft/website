## Overview

When a blog post is published, a comment section opens automatically. Community members at **Traveler level and above** can leave comments. Some comments need moderation before they appear publicly -- that's where you come in.

## Who Can Moderate Comments

- **Blog Authors** (anyone with the Blog Author role)
- **Moderators** (anyone with the Moderator role)
- **Admins**

## How Comment Moderation Works

Not every comment goes through moderation. The system uses a trust-based approach:

| Member Type | Moderation Required? |
|---|---|
| **Citizens** | No -- comments appear immediately |
| **Residents** (6+ months) | No -- comments appear immediately |
| **Residents** (less than 6 months) | Yes -- held for review |
| **Travelers** | Yes -- held for review |

Members in the Brig can't comment at all.

## Where to Find Pending Comments

Pending comments show up in the **Moderation Queue** on the Discussions page:

1. Go to the [Discussions]({{url:/topics}}) page
2. Click the **Moderation Queue** tab
3. If there are pending comments, you'll see a count badge on the tab

Each pending comment shows:
- The commenter's name and avatar
- The comment text
- Which blog post it's on
- **Approve** and **Reject** buttons

## Reviewing a Comment

For each pending comment, you have two choices:

### Approving a Comment

Click **Approve** to make the comment visible on the blog post. The comment will appear in the post's comment section for everyone to see.

### Rejecting a Comment

Click **Reject** to permanently remove the comment. Rejected comments are deleted entirely -- they can't be recovered. Use this for spam, inappropriate content, or comments that violate community rules.

## Tips for Moderating

- **Check the Moderation Queue regularly.** Commenters don't know their comment is being held, so long waits can be frustrating.
- **When in doubt, approve.** If a comment is borderline but not harmful, lean toward approving. You can always discuss community standards with the team.
- **Look at context.** Click through to the blog post to understand what the commenter is responding to before making a decision.
- **Spam is obvious -- remove it.** If it's clearly spam or gibberish, reject without hesitation.

## What Members See

Members whose comments need moderation don't see a "pending" message. Their comment simply won't appear on the post until it's approved. If rejected, the comment disappears as if it was never posted.

Members with high enough trust (Citizens and long-standing Residents) will see their comments appear right away without any moderation step.
