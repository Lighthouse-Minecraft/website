## Overview

Blog posts go through a review pipeline before they reach the community. This keeps quality high and makes sure another set of eyes sees every post before it goes live. You can't publish your own posts -- another Blog Author (or Admin) needs to approve them.

## Post Statuses

Every post has one of these statuses:

| Status | Color | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Draft** | Gray | Work in progress. Only visible on the management page. |
| **In Review** | Amber | Submitted for peer review. Waiting for another Blog Author to approve. |
| **Scheduled** | Blue | Approved, but set to publish at a future date and time. |
| **Published** | Green | Live on the blog. Visible to everyone. |
| **Archived** | Red | Removed from the public blog listing but still accessible by direct link. |

## The Publishing Pipeline

Here's the typical lifecycle of a blog post:

### 1. Write Your Draft

Create a new post on the Blog Management page. Take your time -- drafts are only visible to Blog Authors on the management page. You can save and come back to edit as many times as you need.

### 2. Submit for Review

When your post is ready:

1. Find your draft in the **Posts** tab
2. Click the **Submit for Review** button in the Actions column

The post status changes to **In Review**. All other Blog Authors receive a notification letting them know there's a post waiting for review.

You can only submit your own drafts. If a post is already past the draft stage, the submit button won't appear.

### 3. Peer Review and Approval

When you see a post from another author in the **In Review** status:

1. Read through the post by clicking **Edit** to see the full content
2. If it looks good, click the **Approve** button in the Actions column
3. An approval dialog will appear with two options:
   - **Publish immediately** -- the post goes live right away
   - **Schedule for later** -- pick a date and time. The system will automatically publish the post when that time arrives.
4. Confirm your choice

You can't approve your own posts. This is intentional -- every post needs a second pair of eyes.

### 4. Scheduling (Optional)

If you chose to schedule a post during approval:

- The post status changes to **Scheduled** with the date shown in the Scheduled column
- The system checks every minute for posts whose scheduled time has arrived
- When the time comes, the post is published automatically
- The scheduled date/time is based on your timezone setting in your profile

### 5. Publication

When a post is published (immediately or via schedule):

- It appears on the public [Blog]({{url:/blog}}) page
- A **comment thread** is automatically created for the post
- All **Traveler and above** members receive a notification about the new post
- If the post features any community stories, those story authors are notified that their story was featured
- The post gets full **SEO metadata** -- Open Graph tags, Twitter Card tags, and structured data for search engines

### 6. Archiving

If a post is no longer relevant:

1. Find the published post in the Posts tab
2. Click the **Archive** button

Archived posts are removed from the public blog listing but remain accessible by direct URL. Any Blog Author can archive any published post.

## Important Rules

- **You can't skip the review.** Drafts must be submitted for review and approved by a different author before publication.
- **You can't approve your own posts.** Another Blog Author or Admin needs to handle approval.
- **Editing a published post** marks it with an "Edited" badge, but doesn't require re-review.
- **Admins bypass review** and can approve any post, including their own.

## Notifications Summary

| Event | Who Gets Notified |
|---|---|
| Post submitted for review | All other Blog Authors |
| Post approved | The original author |
| Post published | All Traveler+ members |
| Community story featured | The story's author |
