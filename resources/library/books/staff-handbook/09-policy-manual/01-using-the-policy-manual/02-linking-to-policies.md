## Why Link Directly to Policy Pages

When you answer a member's question by referencing a community policy, sharing a direct link is more useful than paraphrasing. The Policy Manual is public — parents, prospective members, and anyone else can access it without logging in. Sending a link gives the member the authoritative source they can read themselves, and it removes ambiguity about what the policy actually says.

## URL Structure

Policy Manual pages follow this URL pattern:

```text
/library/books/policy-manual/{part}/{chapter}
```

Each chapter contains its full policy content. The Staff & Operations part also has sub-pages within chapters:

```text
/library/books/policy-manual/staff-and-operations/{chapter}/{page}
```

The slugs are derived from the directory names with numeric prefixes stripped. Here are the direct URLs for each policy:

## Useful Scenarios

**Member asks why they were put in the Brig** — Link to The Brig. It explains what behaviors trigger Brig placement and how appeals work.

**Member asks how to report another user** — Link to Moderation Practices. It covers the report process, what information to include, and what to expect.

**Parent asks about child safety** — Link to Child Safety Policy. This page is specifically written to address parent concerns and is written in plain, accessible language.

**Prospective member asks what behavior is expected** — Link to Code of Conduct and/or Community Expectations.

**Member applies for staff and asks what is expected** — Link to Staff Requirements. It outlines conduct standards, confidentiality expectations, and how staff can be removed.

**Member asks how policies are made or updated** — Link to Operational Policies. It describes the policy creation process and how members can raise concerns.

## The Policy Manual Is Authoritative

If there is a conflict between what a staff member says verbally and what is written in the Policy Manual, the manual is the authoritative source. If you believe a policy page is out of date or inaccurate, raise it with Command rather than telling members something different from what is written.
