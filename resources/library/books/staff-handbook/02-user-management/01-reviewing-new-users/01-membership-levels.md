## Overview

Every user on the Lighthouse website has a **membership level** that determines what they can access. Understanding these levels is essential for knowing when and why to promote someone.

## The Levels

Members progress through five levels in order. They cannot skip levels.

| Level | Who | What They Can Access |
|---|---|---|
| **Drifter** | Brand new accounts | Almost nothing -- they need to accept the community rules first |
| **Stowaway** | Accepted the rules | Basic site access, can link a Discord account. This is the "waiting room" where staff reviews them |
| **Traveler** | Approved by staff | Minecraft server access, can link Minecraft accounts, full community updates. Has some limitations to in-game features. |
| **Resident** | Established member | All Traveler access plus resident-level perks |
| **Citizen** | Long-standing member | Highest membership level with full access |

## How Users Move Through Levels

- **Drifter to Stowaway** -- happens automatically when the user reads and accepts the community rules. No staff action needed.
- **Stowaway to Traveler** -- requires staff review and manual promotion. This is your primary review task.
- **Traveler to Resident** -- requires staff promotion after the member has been active for a period of time.
- **Resident to Citizen** -- requires staff promotion for long-standing, trusted members.

## What Syncs on Promotion

When you promote a user, the system automatically:

- Updates their **Minecraft rank** to match their new level (Traveler, Resident, or Citizen)
- Updates their **Discord roles** to match
- Sends the user a **notification** letting them know they've been promoted (for Traveler and Resident promotions)
- Logs the action in the **activity log**

For Stowaway promotions specifically, the notification goes to Quartermaster and Command staff instead of the user, since it's an internal alert that a new user has accepted the rules.
