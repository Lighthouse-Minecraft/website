## What Are Contact Inquiries?

The **Contact Inquiries** queue collects submissions from the public **Contact Us** form at [Contact]({{url:/contact}}). Anyone can fill out that form -- they don't need a Lighthouse account. You'll see questions about membership, concerns from parents, and general outreach land here.

When someone submits the form, they get a confirmation email with a link to view your replies in their browser. They never need to create an account to follow the conversation.

## Who Has Access

Access to the Contact Inquiries queue requires the **Contact - Receive Submissions** role on your staff position. If you don't see **Contact Inquiries** in the Management section of the sidebar, you haven't been assigned that role yet -- ask your department lead or an admin.

Admins always have access regardless of role assignment.

## Viewing the Queue

1. Click **Contact Inquiries** in the sidebar under Management.
2. You'll see the **Open** filter by default, which shows all inquiries that haven't been closed.
3. Click **Closed** to see inquiries that have been fully closed out.

Each row shows the subject, category, the guest's name and email, how long ago the last message arrived, and the current status. A blue **New** badge appears on any inquiry with activity you haven't read yet.

Click any row to open the full conversation.

## Reading an Inquiry

At the top of the inquiry you'll see the subject, category, guest name, guest email, current status, and when the inquiry was submitted.

Messages in the thread are color-coded:

- **Blue background** -- a message from the guest
- **Zinc/grey background** -- a reply from a staff member; a blue **Emailed to guest** badge appears if the guest was notified by email
- **Amber background** -- an internal note (only staff can see these)

## Replying to a Guest

1. Open the inquiry.
2. In the composer at the bottom, make sure the toggle is set to **Reply** (not Internal Note).
3. Decide whether to check the **Email guest** checkbox. When checked, the guest will receive your reply by email with a link back to the conversation. Leave it checked unless you have a specific reason not to.
4. Type your message in the text area.
5. Click **Send Reply**.

The guest will see your reply on their conversation page and, if you left the email option on, in their inbox as well.

## Adding an Internal Note

Internal notes are visible only to staff. Use them to document your thinking, flag something for a colleague, or leave context for whoever handles the inquiry next.

1. Open the inquiry.
2. In the composer, click the **Internal Note** toggle.
3. Type your note.
4. Click **Add Note**.

The **Email guest** option disappears when you switch to internal note mode -- notes are never sent to the guest.

## Changing the Status

The status controls at the top of the inquiry let you move it through its lifecycle. Select the status you want from the dropdown, then click **Update Status**.

See [[books/staff-handbook/support-and-moderation/contact-inquiries/status-reference|Contact Inquiry Status Reference]] for guidance on when to use each status.

When a thread is **Closed** or **Resolved**, the reply composer is hidden. To re-open a thread and respond, change the status back to **Open** or **Pending** first, then reply.

## Tips for Good Handling

- Reply promptly -- guests don't have an account and can't see any internal activity, so silence feels like being ignored.
- Keep replies friendly and professional. Remember that parents and prospective members are often the ones writing in.
- Use **Pending** when you're waiting on something before you can give a final answer.
- Close the thread once the issue is fully resolved and you don't expect further replies.
- If a guest eventually creates a Lighthouse account with the same email address, the system links their inquiry to their account automatically.
