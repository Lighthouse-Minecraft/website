## Overview

When members submit stories, they go into a review queue. Nothing is visible to the community until you approve it. This gives you control over what appears on the public stories page.

## Who Can Do This

Chaplain department staff (Jr Crew and above), Command Officers, and Admins.

## Where to Find Pending Responses

1. Go to the [Community Stories]({{url:/community-stories}}) page
2. Click the **Manage** tab
3. Click the **Pending Responses** sub-tab

You'll see a table of all submitted responses waiting for review, ordered by submission date. A badge on the sub-tab shows the count of pending items.

## Reviewing a Single Response

Click on a response row to open the **review modal**. From there you can:

- **Read** the full response text
- **Edit** the response text if minor corrections are needed (typos, formatting)
- **Approve** the response to make it publicly visible
- **Reject** the response to remove it from the queue

## Bulk Moderation

For faster processing, you can approve or reject multiple responses at once:

1. Use the checkboxes to select responses (or check the header checkbox to select all)
2. Click **Approve Selected** or **Reject Selected**

The system will skip any responses that have already been reviewed.

## What Happens When You Approve

- The response status changes to **Approved**
- Your name and the review time are recorded
- The response immediately appears on the public stories page
- It also shows on the member's profile page

## What Happens When You Reject

- The response status changes to **Rejected**
- Your name and the review time are recorded
- The response is not visible to anyone except the member who submitted it

## Important Notes

- Members can only submit **one response per question**, so rejecting a response means that member won't have a story for that question (unless you ask them to resubmit via a support ticket)
- Responses can include images. Check images before approving to make sure they're appropriate.
- The member is **not** automatically notified when their response is approved or rejected. If you reject a response and want the member to know why, reach out to them directly.
- If you edit a response in the review modal, the changes are saved immediately. This is useful for minor corrections -- for major issues, consider rejecting and asking the member to resubmit.
