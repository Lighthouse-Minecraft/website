## Overview

Discipline reports document behavioral incidents (and commendations) involving community members. Every staff member can create reports, which start as drafts until an Officer publishes them.

## Who Can Do This

All staff members (Junior Crew and above) can create and view discipline reports.

## How to Create a Report

1. Navigate to the member's **profile page**
2. Scroll to the **Discipline Reports** card
3. Click **New Report**
4. Fill in the report form:
   - **Description** -- what happened (minimum 10 characters). Supports Markdown formatting.
   - **Location** -- where it occurred: Minecraft, Discord Text, Discord Voice, or Other
   - **Actions Taken** -- what you did in response (minimum 5 characters). Supports Markdown.
   - **Severity** -- how serious the incident was (see below)
   - **Category** -- optional tag like Language, Harassment, Griefing, etc.
   - **Rules Violated** -- optional. Select any Community Rules that were broken. Members can see these on the published report.
5. Click **Save**

The report is saved as a **Draft**. It won't be visible to the member until an Officer publishes it.

## Severity Levels

Choose the severity level that best matches the situation:

| Severity | Points | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Commendation** | 0 | Positive behavior worth recognizing |
| **Trivial** | 1 | Very minor issue, barely worth noting |
| **Minor** | 2 | Small rule violation, first-time offense |
| **Moderate** | 4 | Repeated minor issues or a more notable violation |
| **Major** | 7 | Serious rule violation |
| **Severe** | 10 | Extremely serious incident |

Severity points feed into the member's **risk score**, which helps staff identify patterns over time.

## Categories

Categories are tags that help classify what type of incident occurred. Common categories include Language, Harassment, Griefing, Cheating, Disrespect, Spam, and Inappropriate Content. Officers can manage categories from the Admin Control Panel.

## Important Notes

- Reports are **not** discipline actions themselves -- they're documentation. Use the [[books/staff-handbook/user-management/the-brig/putting-users-in-the-brig|Brig]] for restricting access.
- Write factual, specific descriptions. Avoid emotional language -- these reports may be seen by the member and their parents.
- You can edit your own draft reports until they're published. After publication, reports cannot be edited.
