## Overview

When a ticket comes in, you'll review it, discuss with staff if needed, reply to the member, and close it when the issue is resolved. Here's how each step works.

## Finding Tickets

Go to **Tickets** from the navigation bar. You'll see a filterable list with these views:

- **My Open** -- open tickets you're a participant in
- **Assigned to Me** -- tickets specifically assigned to you
- **Open / Closed** -- all tickets in your visible scope by status
- **Unassigned** -- open tickets nobody has claimed yet
- **Flagged** -- tickets with flagged messages (Quartermaster only)

Unread tickets are marked so you can quickly spot new activity.

## Replying to a Ticket

1. Open the ticket from the list
2. Read through the conversation
3. Type your reply in the message box at the bottom
4. Click **Send**

Your reply is visible to all ticket participants, including the member who created it. Keep replies professional, clear, and helpful.

## Internal Notes

**Internal notes** are messages that only staff can see. The member will never see them. Use internal notes to:

- Discuss how to handle a situation with other staff
- Document decisions or context for the team
- Leave notes for the next person who picks up the ticket

To post an internal note, toggle the **Internal Note** switch before sending your message. Internal notes appear with a distinct style so staff can tell them apart from regular replies.

**Who can use internal notes:** Crew Members and above.

## Changing Ticket Status

Staff can change a ticket's status using the status controls on the ticket page. Common workflows:

- **Open to Pending** -- you're waiting on information from the member or another staff member
- **Open/Pending to Closed** -- the issue is resolved and you're closing it out
- A system message is posted to the conversation when the status changes so everyone can see the update

## Assigning and Rerouting

**Officers** can:

- **Assign** a ticket to a specific staff member -- click the assign control and select who should handle it
- **Reroute** a ticket to a different department -- use this when a ticket was sent to the wrong department

## Creating Admin Action Tickets

Sometimes you need to create a ticket **about** a member rather than waiting for them to reach out. Admin action tickets let you document situations proactively.

1. Go to **Tickets** and click **Create Admin Ticket** (Crew Member+ only)
2. Select the **department** and **subject user**
3. Write your message describing the situation
4. The subject user becomes a participant and can see the ticket

## Message Flagging

Any ticket participant can **flag** a message they find concerning. When a message is flagged:

1. A new **moderation flag ticket** is automatically created in the Quartermaster department
2. Quartermaster staff are notified
3. The original ticket is marked as flagged

Quartermaster staff review the flag, add staff notes, and acknowledge it. This is a safety net for catching inappropriate content in ticket conversations.

## Important Notes

- Notifications are sent to participants when someone replies -- be mindful that your reply may trigger an email
- Members can choose to receive ticket notifications immediately or as daily/weekly digests
- Parents can see their children's tickets, so keep your responses appropriate and professional
- Brigged users can still see and reply to tickets they're already part of, but can't create new ones
