## Overview

Support tickets are how members ask for help, report problems, or request actions. The ticket system is your primary tool for communicating with members about their issues. Tickets are organized by department, have statuses and assignments, and support internal staff-only notes.

## Ticket Types

There are three types of tickets you'll encounter:

- **Support tickets** -- created by members who need help. These are the most common.
- **Admin action tickets** -- created by staff (Crew Member+) about a specific member. Use these to document actions you've taken or issues you've noticed.
- **Moderation flag tickets** -- created automatically when someone flags a message in another ticket. These go to the Quartermaster department for review.

## Ticket Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Open** | New or active -- needs attention |
| **Pending** | Waiting on something (a response, a decision, etc.) |
| **Resolved** | The member marked it as resolved |
| **Closed** | Staff closed the ticket |

Members can mark their own tickets as "resolved." Staff can close any ticket they have access to.

## Who Can See What

Your access to tickets depends on your rank and department:

| Role | What You Can See |
|---|---|
| **Crew Member** | Your own department's tickets |
| **Officer** | Your own department's tickets |
| **Quartermaster Crew Member+** | Department tickets plus flagged tickets from any department |
| **Command Officer / Admin** | All tickets across all departments |

Everyone can always see tickets they created or are a participant in, regardless of department.

## Ticket Assignments

Officers can **assign** tickets to specific staff members. Assigned tickets appear in the "Assigned to Me" filter. Assignments help the team know who's handling what so work doesn't get duplicated. If a staff member (who is not the ticket creator) posts a non-internal reply on an unassigned ticket, that staff member will be automatically assigned to the ticket.

Officers can also **reroute** tickets to a different department if they were sent to the wrong one.
