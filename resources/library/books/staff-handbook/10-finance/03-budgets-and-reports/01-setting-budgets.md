## Overview

The **Budgets** page lets you set monthly budget amounts for every revenue and expense account across a fiscal year. You can also compare budgets to actual posted amounts, and copy the prior year's budgets as a starting point for a new year.

Viewing budgets requires **Finance - View**. Editing budget amounts requires **Finance - Manage**.

## Getting There

Click **Budgets** in the Finance nav (only visible to Finance - Manage users, but Finance - View users can navigate directly to `/finance/budgets`).

## Selecting a Fiscal Year

Use the **FY selector** in the top-right to switch between fiscal years. The page defaults to the current fiscal year.

## Entering Budget Amounts

The **Budget Entry** tab shows a grid with accounts as rows and months as columns. Each cell is an editable field (Finance - Manage only).

1. Click a cell to edit it
2. Type the dollar amount for that account and month
3. Tab or click away — the amount saves automatically
4. The **FY Total** column on the right shows the full-year sum for each account

Leave a cell blank or enter zero for months where that account has no budgeted amount.

## Budget vs. Actual Tab

Switch to the **Budget vs. Actual** tab to see actual posted amounts alongside the budget:

- **Budget** column — What was budgeted for the month
- **Actual** column — What was actually recorded (posted entries only; drafts excluded)
- Color coding: **green** means favorable (revenue came in above budget, or expenses came in under budget); **red** means unfavorable (revenue below target, or expenses over budget)

## Copying Budgets from the Prior Year

At the start of a new fiscal year, you can pull last year's budget amounts as a starting point:

1. Select the **target fiscal year** (the year you want to populate) using the FY selector
2. Click **Copy from FY [prior year]**
3. The current year's budget grid is populated with last year's amounts. You can then adjust any cells that should be different.

If the prior year has no budgets, you'll get a toast saying nothing was copied.

## Important Notes

- Budget amounts don't affect accounting — they're planning data only. The Finance System tracks actuals separately through journal entries.
- You can update budget amounts at any time, including mid-year.
- Copying from a prior year overwrites any existing budget amounts for the target year. Do this before entering any new amounts, or be aware that it will replace what's there.
