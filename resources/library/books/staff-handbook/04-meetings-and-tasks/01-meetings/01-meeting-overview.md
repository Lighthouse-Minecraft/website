## Overview

The meeting system manages the full lifecycle of staff meetings -- from scheduling through note-taking to publishing community updates. Meetings progress through defined stages and support collaborative note-taking, attendance tracking, and pre-meeting check-in reports.

## Meeting Types

| Type | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Staff Meeting** | Regular staff meetings with department notes, check-in reports, and community updates |
| **Board Meeting** | Board of Directors meetings |
| **Community Meeting** | Open community meetings |
| **Other** | Anything that doesn't fit the above |

Staff meetings get special treatment: they have department-specific note sections, check-in questions, and their notes can be published as community updates.

## Meeting Lifecycle

Every meeting moves through these stages:

1. **Pending** -- the meeting is scheduled. Staff can submit check-in reports during this phase.
2. **In Progress** -- the meeting has started. Staff collaborate on notes in real time.
3. **Finalizing** -- the meeting has ended. Minutes are compiled and community notes can be formatted.
4. **Completed** -- the meeting is archived and viewable. Community updates are published if enabled.

Meetings can also be **Cancelled**.

## Who Can Manage Meetings

- Users with the **Manage Staff Meeting** role can create, start, end, and finalize meetings, manage attendees, and edit all note sections
- **Officers** can edit meeting notes for their own department's section
- **Crew Members** and above can view meetings, attend, and submit check-in reports

## Where to Find Meetings

Upcoming meetings appear in the **Staff Ready Room** on the "My Board" tab and on each department tab. You'll see the next few upcoming meetings with their scheduled time and whether you've submitted your check-in report.
