Lighthouse staff are the people responsible for leading, managing, and maintaining the community. Because they hold authority over other members, staff are held to higher standards than the general membership. This chapter describes those standards publicly so that members — and prospective staff — understand what is expected.

## Purpose of the Staff Team

Staff members serve the community. Their role is to:

- Maintain a safe, welcoming, and well-run environment for all members.
- Enforce community policies fairly and consistently.
- Support members who need help or have questions.
- Contribute to the community's continued growth and improvement.

Staff authority exists in service of the community, not for personal benefit. Abuse of staff authority is treated as a serious violation.

## Minimum Requirements for Staff Membership

Candidates for staff roles must meet the following baseline requirements:

- **Established membership.** Staff candidates must have been active Lighthouse members for a meaningful period of time. Newly registered members are not eligible for staff roles.
- **Good standing.** Candidates must not be in the Brig or have a recent history of disciplinary action.
- **Demonstrated conduct.** Candidates should have demonstrated the community's values through their behavior over time — not just in the weeks before applying.
- **Age.** Staff members are expected to have the maturity appropriate for their role, particularly when the role involves interacting with and making decisions about minors.

Final decisions on staff eligibility are made by Command, who may apply additional criteria based on the specific role being filled.

## Conduct Standards for Staff

Staff members are expected to:

- **Model the community's values.** Staff behavior sets the tone for the community. In all community spaces, staff are visible examples of what is expected of members.
- **Act with integrity.** Staff do not use their position for personal gain, to favor friends, or to punish people they dislike.
- **Remain impartial.** When moderating or making decisions, staff set aside personal relationships and evaluate situations on their merits.
- **Communicate professionally.** Staff interact with members — including during tense situations — with patience and clarity.
- **Maintain confidentiality.** Staff regularly handle sensitive information about members. That information is not shared outside of appropriate staff channels.

Violations of these standards are handled by Command.

## Confidentiality Expectations

Staff members have access to information about members that is not available to the general community — disciplinary records, support ticket contents, account information. Staff are expected to:

- Keep this information strictly confidential.
- Not discuss it with non-staff members.
- Not use it for purposes outside of their staff role.

Breaches of confidentiality are treated as serious misconduct.

## Joining and Leaving the Staff Team

**Joining staff:** Individuals interested in joining the staff team may apply through the [Staff Applications](/applications) page. Applications are reviewed by Command and the relevant department heads. Not all applications are accepted.

**Leaving staff:** Staff members who wish to step down may do so voluntarily at any time by informing Command. Staff who step down in good standing may reapply in the future.

**Removal from staff:** Staff members may be removed from the team if they:
- Consistently fail to meet the conduct standards described in this policy
- Abuse their staff authority
- Become inactive for an extended period without communication
- Engage in behavior that would result in disciplinary action for any member

Removal from staff may also result in a Brig placement or removal from the community depending on the nature of the conduct.

## Addressing Staff Misconduct

If a member believes a staff member has acted improperly, they should:

1. Submit a [Support Ticket](/tickets/create) describing the concern.
2. Address the ticket to Command.
3. Include specific details about what occurred, when, and how.

Concerns about staff misconduct are handled by Command. If the concern involves a Command member, members may reach out to the lead administrator directly.

Raising concerns about staff conduct in good faith will never be penalized.
