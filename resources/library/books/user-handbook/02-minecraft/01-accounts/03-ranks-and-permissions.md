## Your Rank Comes from the Website

Your in-game rank on the Lighthouse Minecraft server is tied directly to your **membership level** on the website. You don't need to do anything special in-game to earn a rank -- it happens automatically when staff promotes you on the website.

When your membership level changes, your in-game rank updates right away. No commands needed, no waiting around.

## The Ranks

Here's how website membership levels map to in-game ranks:

| Website Level | In-Game Rank | Server Access |
|---|---|---|
| **Drifter** | None | No access |
| **Stowaway** | None | No access |
| **Traveler** | Traveler | Full access |
| **Resident** | Resident | Full access |
| **Citizen** | Citizen | Full access |

**Drifters** and **Stowaways** can't link their Minecraft accounts yet.

## What Each Rank Gets

### Traveler

This is your first in-game rank. This is a slightly limited account that allows you to do most things on the server as we get to know you. Once you have shown that you fit into the community and have not caused issues, then you can be promoted to Resident.

### Resident

**Residents** are established members of the community. This rank is given to members who have been active and have shown they're a great part of the Lighthouse family. Residents have a few extra perks within the community, such as access to the `/fly` command and the ability to create more lands for yourself.

### Citizen

**Citizen** is the highest membership level. It's reserved for long-standing, trusted members of the community that have proven to be an exceptional part of the community. Citizens may have access to special perks or privileges on the server. This rank is reserved for those who have gone above and beyond a normal community member within the community.

## Staff Ranks

If you're a staff member on the Lighthouse website, your staff position is also reflected in-game. This helps other players know who to turn to if they need help while playing and provides the in-game permissions required to perform staff duties.

## How Promotions Work

You don't need to apply for a promotion -- staff members regularly review the community and promote members when they're ready. The best way to move up is to be an active, positive member of the community. Promotions happen naturally over time, so just enjoy playing and being part of the group.

If you've been around for a while and are curious about your membership level, you can check it on your **profile page** on the website.
