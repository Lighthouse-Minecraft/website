## Overview

After you submit a staff application, you can check on its progress at any time. You'll also receive email notifications whenever your application status changes.

## Viewing Your Applications

1. Go to [My Applications]({{url:/applications}})
2. You'll see a list of all applications you've submitted, with the position name, department, current status, and the date you applied
3. Click on any application to see its full details

## Understanding Application Statuses

Your application moves through a series of stages as the review team works through it:

| Status | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Submitted** | Your application has been received and is waiting for review |
| **Under Review** | A staff reviewer has started looking at your application |
| **Interview** | You've been selected for an interview -- check your discussions |
| **Background Check** | Your application has moved to the background check stage |
| **Approved** | Congratulations -- your application was accepted! |
| **Denied** | Your application wasn't accepted this time |
| **Withdrawn** | You chose to withdraw your application |

You'll receive an email at each of these stages so you're never left guessing.

## If You're Selected for an Interview

When your application moves to the Interview stage, a **discussion thread** is created for you to communicate with the review team. You'll find a link to this discussion on your application detail page.

Check your discussions regularly during this stage -- the review team may have questions for you or want to schedule a conversation.

## Withdrawing Your Application

Changed your mind? You can withdraw your application at any point before a final decision is made.

1. Go to [My Applications]({{url:/applications}})
2. Click on the application you want to withdraw
3. Click **Withdraw Application** at the bottom of the page
4. Confirm your decision

Once withdrawn, the application is final and can't be reopened. If you change your mind later, you'll need to submit a new application.

## After Approval

If your application is approved, you'll see the approval details on your application page, including any conditions (such as a trial period). You'll also be automatically assigned to the staff position -- welcome to the team!

## If Your Application Is Denied

It's okay to be disappointed, but don't let it discourage you. The review team considers many factors, and a denial now doesn't mean you can't apply again in the future. Take some time, stay active in the community, and consider applying again when a position opens up.
