## What You Can See

If you've had a background check run as part of a staff application, you can view your own check history directly on your profile page. This gives you a clear record of when checks were run, which service was used, and what the current status is.

You can also download any documents that have been attached to your records, such as the check report itself.

## Finding Your Records

1. Go to your profile page -- click your name in the top navigation bar to get there
2. Scroll down to the **Background Checks** section
3. You'll see a list of all checks on file, sorted with the most recent first

If no checks have been run yet, you'll see a message indicating there are no records on file.

## Reading a Check Record

Each record shows:

- **Status** -- the current result of the check (Pending, Passed, Failed, Waived, etc.)
- **Service** -- which background check provider was used
- **Date** -- when the check was completed
- **Run by** -- which staff member coordinated the check
- **Notes** -- any notes added by the review team
- **Documents** -- attached files you can download

A check with a **Passed**, **Failed**, or **Waived** status is considered complete and the record is locked -- the status won't change from that point.

## Statuses Explained

| Status | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | The check has been initiated but isn't complete yet |
| **Deliberating** | The review team is evaluating the results |
| **Passed** | The background check came back clear |
| **Failed** | The check did not pass |
| **Waived** | A background check was not required for this position |

## What You Can't Do

Your view of background check records is read-only. You can't add notes, change statuses, or upload documents -- those actions are handled by staff with the appropriate role. If you have a question about a specific record, open a [[books/user-handbook/website/getting-help/support-tickets|support ticket]].

## Troubleshooting

### I don't see a Background Checks section on my profile

This section only appears if you're viewing your own profile while logged in, or if a staff member with the right access is viewing your profile. If you expect to have records on file but don't see the section, make sure you're logged in and viewing your own profile.

### A status looks incorrect

If you believe a record has an error, reach out to staff through a [[books/user-handbook/website/getting-help/support-tickets|support ticket]] so they can look into it.
