## What Are Community Standards?

The **Community Standards** section of the Policy Manual sets out the behavioral baseline for everyone in Lighthouse. These aren't just rules on paper -- they reflect the kind of community we're actively working to build together.

There are two chapters in this section:

- **Code of Conduct** -- The specific behaviors that are required or prohibited in all Lighthouse spaces (the server, Discord, and the website).
- **Community Expectations** -- The broader values and culture that Lighthouse expects members to contribute to.

## The Short Version

Lighthouse is a faith-based, family-friendly community. That means:

- Treat everyone with respect and kindness.
- Keep language and content clean -- children are part of this community.
- Don't grief, steal, cheat, or harass other players.
- Disagree graciously -- debate is welcome, cruelty is not.

For the full details, read the [[books/policy-manual/community-standards/code-of-conduct|Code of Conduct]] in the Policy Manual.

## What Happens If You Break the Rules?

Violations are handled by staff. Depending on the situation, consequences might include a warning, a temporary placement in the [[books/user-handbook/website/the-brig/what-is-the-brig|Brig]], or removal from the community for serious violations.

Staff try to be fair and consider context, but there are some behaviors -- like hate speech or harassment -- where the response is immediate and firm.

## New Members

If you're new to Lighthouse, the Community Standards section is worth a quick read before you dive in. Knowing what's expected helps you get settled without surprises, and it makes the community better for everyone.
